  • Tips & tricks  video

    • WRITE A SCRIPT OR OUTLINE

      Plan out what to show on the screen. Knowing what you will show before you create the video will save a lot of time and frustration.

    • KEEP IT SIMPLE STUPID!

      Make sure you have a clear goal for the content of the video. Try to focus on recording one idea or theme per video. Doing too much will complicate the video message. Viewers will lose focus if the video skips around or the video is too long.

    • PRACTICE MAKES PERFECT

      Practice is the key to creating professional videos. It takes time to learn how to frame each shot, memorize and recite lines, and how to edit your videos.

    • STORY BOARDING

      If you are creating an action video or a video with a lot of camera cuts, sketching out the scene beforehand of camera framing and movements is crucial for both the actors as well as the camera operator.

    • COMPOSITION

      Composition is otherwise known as framing each shot. Composition is really important especially when you are shooting solo and do not have a camera operator to assist you. After all, you would hate to get the perfect take only to find out that your head was not in the frame.

    • CAMERAS

      You don't need fancy or expensive equipment to make a great video! If you can't afford any equipment, you can always borrow a camera from a friend, use your cell phone (make sure it's horizontal of course!), or rent one from stores such as National Camera Exchange.

    tips & tricks audio

    • right mic for the job

      If you have more than one mic, having the right microphone positioned in the right spot makes all the difference in the world to the quality of your audio. 

    • copyright laws

      Due to copyright laws set in place to protect musicians, composers, and publishers, only use music found in the public domain, creative commons, or music you have created yourself. You video will be flagged, taken down, and your account will become suspended after 3 copyright strikes. 

    • location, location, location!

      Remember to always scope out your shooting locations in advance when possible. Try to bring the camera and microphone that you'll be shooting with and a good pair of headphones to identify problem noise sources.

    • outdoor microphones

      If you are shooting outside, make sure you have adequate wind and weather protection for your microphone. Foam windscreens may not be adequate for any type of real breeze, so you should consider heavier wind and rain protection to insure better results when shooting outside.

    • the perfect ratio

      Generally speaking, your music levels should be from right around 10% - 20% in terms of loudness and your dialogue should be around 80% - 90%.

    • audio program

      Consider using a dedicated audio application. A great free program I would highly recommend downloading is Audacity.
